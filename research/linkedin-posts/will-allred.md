## 1. ~2026-06-24 — Buyer's-perspective pyramid: demand creation > demand capture

> Sellers, this pyramid is your market. Most prospecting messaging is optimized for the green — people "buying now" / "open to it." If you'd stop talking about yourself, you'd 3x your opportunity zone.
>
> The biggest mental flip is that it's the *buyer's* perspective: "I'm buying now / I'm open to it / I'm not thinking about you / I'm not interested." You're just an interruption in their mindset.
>
> You can get a lot from very targeted messaging that boils your pitch to a hyper-simple "We do X. Solves Y. Interested?" — but your ceiling is capped. **Demand creation > capture** is how you get out of that cap: "If ___ is true, would it help if you could ____?" Pain is the name of the game. Validate that it exists, then carry the conversation forward.

*Why it matters:* most cold email only targets the ~5% in-market; leading with a validated
pain reaches the much larger "not thinking about you" segment.

## 2. ~2026-06-17 — When cold-email personalization is (and isn't) worth it

> Cold email personalization doesn't always "make sense" — it depends on the target audience, the price point, and the deal cycle length.
>
> Selling EHR software to a big retailer (Walmart/Target/CVS)? Make that email 1:1 — the deal size and stickiness are worth figuring out. But the SMB team reaching small independent practices? You could name-drop and personalize hypotheses about their workflow pains… but having tested it, the juice isn't worth the squeeze.
>
> You're better off investing heavily in **product marketing**: who typically closes, who's involved, what they care about, how they talk about it, their usual doubts and motivations. Then it's about how you craft the message — breaking patterns, making it easy to understand, easy to respond to.

*Why it matters:* a rare, data-backed take on personalization ROI — segment by deal
size/cycle rather than personalizing everything.

## 3. ~2026-06-24 — Protect top accounts from the "spam cannon"

> Your spam cannon needs to stay *FAR* away from your top accounts. The deal is worth more than $50k?… and you can't even take the time to review the copy?! What are we even doing? (I presented this slide on a webinar 2 years ago — still relevant.)

*Why it matters:* tier your outreach — automate low-value volume, hand-craft tier-1
