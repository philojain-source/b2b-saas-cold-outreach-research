## 1. ~2026-07-07 — Entire cold call, discovery, and multithreading playbook in 457 words

> **COLD CALLING**
>
> *Heard-The-Name-Tossed-Around Opener:* "Hey Jane, not sure if you knew but your team's full of 30MPC super fans. It's Armand from 30MPC, heard our name tossed around?"
>
> *Problem Proposition:* "This might totally be off, but a lot of enablement leaders are asked to onboard every rep, lead every product training, train reps on multithreading... and cure world hunger. We've got a program that teaches your reps anything from cold calling to discovery to negotiation... AND we help you build all the playbooks so you don't have to cure world hunger on your own. Open to seeing an example program?"

> **DISCOVERY**
>
> *Use a POV to drop into a SITUATION:* "Your team dl'd our disco cheatsheet and you're moving upmarket. That usually means you're transitioning to a value sale beyond surface-level discovery, but where am I off?"
>
> *Then a multiple-choice question to get an OPERATIONAL PROBLEM:* "When teams struggle with discovery, it's either depth or breadth. They don't get DEEP enough problems or they don't get WIDE enough across products. Which is it for you?"
>
> *Then a magic-moment question to get Business Impact:* "This might be an awkward question, but when'd you realize this was a problem? Was it a deal that got blown up at the finish line or just something you've seen call after call?"

>
> *Ask "Will you be my friend?":* get on the same side of the table to get the VP and other directors onboard. *Map the committee and eat a champion sandwich:* get 1:1 with your champion and map the buying committee; meet before + after each call to prep. *Initiate the Recap and Roll:* for every new stakeholder, RECAP the problems you've learned and ask them to ROLL in their perspective (builds on discovery without starting over).

*Why it matters:* a complete, copy-able top-of-funnel script — opener → problem
proposition → layered discovery questions.

## 2. ~2026-07-08 — The "Pimp My Ride" demo (discovery-led customization)

> You do all of this discovery. And then give the exact same golden-path demo with the same stories, same single-source-of-truth buzzwords, same jargon. Instead, do the pimp-my-ride demo: "You said [thing from discovery] — that's why we have [thing from our product]." It is a forcing function to (A) customize the demo and (B) only demo the parts of your product that are relevant.

*Why it matters:* connects discovery to the pitch — mirror the prospect's own words back
instead of a generic path.
