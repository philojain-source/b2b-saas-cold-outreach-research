## 1. ~2026-06-08 — Strategy vs. execution: outbound dies in the "last 1%"

> The disconnect between sales strategy and sales execution is often in the last 1%.
> Companies do 99% of the work and still fail. Example chain: you aggregate intent data and
> signals and throw it all on your SEP → leads go stale because the team can't sequence 200
> leads/day/rep → you buy parallel dialers → now your emails trigger too quickly and your
> domain reputation gets hurt, and your phone numbers show as "spam likely" → you fix domain
> reputation and enable caller ID → but you're not tracking missed calls in your SEP, so 80%
> of the work didn't return what was expected.
>
> Outbound programs fail over and over because people don't have time to find the next thing
> to fix, and then the next thing. This is what made us successful at AltiSales — we're 10
> steps ahead and have solved the 8th-order-effect problems you'd face starting outbound
> today. At the surface there's no difference between your strategy and ours; once you see
> the execution, it's miles apart. (Like playing tennis with the same strokes as Alcaraz —
> off in a ton of details that took him years to perfect.)

*Why it matters:* the best argument in the set for why outbound is an *execution* game;
each fix creates a downstream problem, and the winners just out-detail everyone.

## 2. ~2026-06-08 — AI is an insane enabler — but now it's about your people

> What used to take an army can now be done by a few. Finding/scoring the TAM went from
> 20–30% of the SDR job to 1 person in Clay + Claude doing it for the whole team. Great notes
> for every prospect → a RevOps-built data pipeline. Coaching on call recordings → most
> calls analyzed by AI immediately for right-on-time feedback. Pipeline management → Claude
> sends a shortlist 2x/week on which opps need more touches or multithreading. Tech-stack
> tuning for connect/reply rates → the click of a button with Claude + Caddy.
>
> The key to success is your people now… A driven, smart rep can produce 10x an average SDR.
> The $250K or $350K SDR is no longer a myth — if they create $1M or $3M in revenue, why
> wouldn't you pay them that?

*Why it matters:* concrete map of which SDR tasks AI absorbs — and the shift toward fewer,
higher-paid, higher-output reps.

## 3. ~2026-07-02 — Tool ROI: don't go cheap on the SEP

> Companies try to save $500/yr per employee on a tool like Outreach and lose 20–40% of
> their productivity ($20,000 per employee). It's the biggest negative ROI a company could
> have… Saved $10K on 20 licenses, lost $400K in productivity, and now you have to rebuild
> the team. Switching from a best-in-class tool to a cheaper one for "consolidation" will
> backfire.

*Why it matters:* the unit-economics case against tooling downgrades — productivity dwarfs
license cost.
