## 1. ~2026-07-07 — Referral reply framework ("I'm not the right person")

> "Thanks for your email, I'm not the right person." → "Got it. Could you point me in the
> right direction of who is?" — those replies are about as standard as they come. Here's a
> better framework, straight out of our deck:
> - 💡 Reply with gratitude
> - 💡 Use EQ in your ask
> - 💡 Offer logic
> - 💡 Anticipate that they may want confidentiality
> - 💡 Close with appreciation
>
> Example:
> "Hi Paige! Thanks for the note back and taking the time to reply. Wonder if you might know
> who a better contact is within the org? I realize it's my job to figure it out but I'd be
> so appreciative of your direction. I'm of course happy to not mention your name, if you'd
> prefer I not, or mention it if you think it would help get a reply from your colleague.
> Thank you for considering and again for even taking the time to reply! Cheers, Sam"
>
> Why does this obvious reply work more often than not? Because the bar is so low in how
> buyers are usually replied to that this is a welcome change. Be human. Don't be lazy.
> Always lead and close with gratitude.

*Why it matters:* turns the most common brush-off ("not the right person") into a warm
internal referral.

## 2. ~2026-07-03 — Sales Navigator "Connections Of" hack (warm-intro machine)

> Using LinkedIn Sales Navigator felt like cheating — here's a hack you can set up in 10
> min that turns it into a lead machine:
> 1. Click **Lead Filters** on the homepage.
> 2. **Connections Of:** your executive team, senior cross-functional leaders, your boss,
>    their peers, etc. (you must be 1st-degree connected, so do that first).
> 3. **Job Title:** the specific titles you're targeting AND senior cross-functional stakeholders.
> 4. **Current Company and/or Saved Accounts:** import your territory via CRM integration, or add your top 50 accounts.
> 5. **Geo:** start with the broader territory you cover.
>
> Save the search and check it weekly — you get a custom report that updates in real time as
> your executives make new connections and as their contacts change jobs. Now you can get
> those leaders to introduce you to your buyers, improving speed-to-meeting and
> speed-to-trust.

*Why it matters:* a repeatable way to source *warm* paths into target accounts instead of
pure cold outreach.
