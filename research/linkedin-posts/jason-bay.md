## 1. ~2026-07-08 — Debugging an SDR team: Behaviors → Systems → Messaging → Skills (w/ Tito Bohrt)

> Tito Bohrt runs AltiSales and has built hundreds of SDR teams… Three big takeaways:
>
> **1. Behaviors. Systems. Messaging. Skills. In that order.** Most leaders work backward — the team isn't booking meetings, so they train on objection handling (skills). But the objections come because the messaging isn't dialed in. And messaging doesn't matter if the connect rate is 1.5% and emails aren't landing (systems). And systems can't be fixed if reps take 72 hours to reply, fake-dial, or hang up the second a prospect says hello (behavior). Run this top to bottom before booking another training session.
>
> **2. 6%+ connect rates** (most teams are 1.5–3%): power-dial one number at a time (no parallel dialing); get through AI screeners (~80%) by calling the same screener days apart, leaving a voicemail between, and changing what you say; use person + company caller ID ("Tito Bohrt" shows on 92% of US numbers); pick in-state vs out-of-state numbers by persona (e.g., NYC area code for finance).
>
> **3. AI will hit AEs before SDRs** — an AE's day is 2–3 hrs of calls and 5 hrs of automatable admin; his prediction: AE headcount halves, quota doubles, saved headcount moves to SDRs.

Video: https://lnkd.in/gUpmXBKV

*Why it matters:* a debugging order for any underperforming outbound team + concrete
connect-rate levers.

## 2. ~2026-06-24 — Building a pipeline-generation (PG) culture with AEs (7 moves)

> We recently helped 300+ AEs at iHeartMedia increase self-sourced meetings 40%+… Self-sourcing is an ATTITUDE. What's working:
> 1. **Train the AI** — load examples of great outbound and process into the LLM as skills; signing up for Claude accounts isn't enough.
> 2. **Up-skill AEs as much as BDRs** — role-play, weekly coaching, phone/email/LinkedIn.
> 3. **Enable front-line leaders** — managers who can't outbound can't coach it.
> 4. **Rub shoulders** — managers make calls with AEs into tier-1 accounts weekly.
> 5. **Arm AEs with the right tools** — same sequencing/dialer access as BDRs.
> 6. **Top-down involvement** — CRO/VP give public shout-outs for outbound-sourced meetings.
> 7. **Data** — best-in-class email + mobile data; you can't dial without it.

*Why it matters:* outbound is a culture/enablement problem, not just a rep-skill problem.

## 3. ~2026-06-24 — Bullish on the phone; against automated spray-and-pray

> "I send 10,000+ cold emails a day, but NOBODY should cold call." … all this "fully automate outbound with this workflow" nonsense is a re-packaged version of spraying and praying. It's still noise, the quality isn't great, and it pisses off buyers. I'm 100% bullish on the phones and in-person right now because it's the least crowded way to get attention. Execs get maybe a dozen cold calls a day; no one's reaching out to meet them in person. Pick up the phone and get after it.

*Why it matters:* channel-selection argument — as email volume automates and degrades,
the phone and in-person become the differentiated channels.
