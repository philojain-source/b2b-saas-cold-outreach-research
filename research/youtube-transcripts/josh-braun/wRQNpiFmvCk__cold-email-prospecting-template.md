## The template (verbatim from the video)

> Hi X,
>
> For the past couple of weeks, I've invested considerable time reviewing GEICO's insurance
> quote and sign-up process (yes, I study this kind of stuff for a living).
>
> I have an idea that might increase the amount of people that complete the sign-up process
> while improving the online customer experience.
>
> You can see a sneak peek here. [link to a short demo/mock-up video]
>
> Feel free to say no, but does this sound interesting?
>
> Regards,
> Josh

**[0:52] The template.** [The GEICO email above.] So we have the problem, the hypothesis,

then demonstrated the solution. Your email outreach does not need to be complex — follow
this step-by-step process. The last step — **sending a demo of how you'd solve the problem**
rather than just describing it — is really key. Have a think about whether any part of the
problem you solve could be *demoed* in an email rather than described.

positions him as an **industry expert** rather than another pesky salesperson; (2) he uses a
soft close — **"does this sound interesting?"** rather than "book a call in my diary" /
"here's a link to my calendar," which is what most people default to.

### Playbook takeaways
- Structure the first cold email as **problem → hypothesis → demonstrated solution**.
- Where possible, **show** (mock-up/loom) instead of **tell**.
- Signal expertise early ("I study this for a living").
- Close soft: ask if it "sounds interesting," giving an easy out.
